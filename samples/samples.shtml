The trizbort files below weren't picked with any special algorithm. However, they do attempt to show some ways that formatting and coloring can be used to create a map that can give hints a straightforward walkthrough can't organize, or maybe bring back memories of critical game parts. Maps can't take the place of walkthroughs, but they can often provide people a sense of what they might've missed, or even how big a game is.

Individual links are to PDF files, since browsers may open XML as text. The entire collection of Trizbort files is here.

Slasher Swamp IFComp 2014 Non-reciprocal exits, various regions, and several one-way exits to a certain room. One feature of the game was the necessity to search various objects, and those rooms are dashed. Numbers in the object space indicate the order to search.
Luster IFComp 2011 A small non-reciprocal maze as well as quest areas that give themselves naturally to regions. Two rooms are tagged to indicate a puzzle in one room helps with another. A one-way and dotted passage show the entrance/exit in a jail. Other formatting includes the color of gems that need to go on various pedestals near the end.
